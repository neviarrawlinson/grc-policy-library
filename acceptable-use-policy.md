## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of organizational information
systems, devices, networks, and data. It establishes the standards
that all personnel must follow when using organizational technology
resources to ensure those resources remain secure, available, and
used for legitimate business purposes.

Acceptable use is not just a compliance requirement. It is a shared
responsibility that protects the organization, its customers, and
every person who depends on its systems to do their work.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All organizational devices — laptops, desktops, mobile devices,
  tablets, and servers
- All organizational networks — on-premises, remote, and cloud
- All organizational applications and software
- All organizational data regardless of where it is stored or
  processed
- All full-time and part-time employees, contractors, consultants,
  temporary staff, and vendors with access to organizational systems

This policy applies whether the user is accessing organizational
systems from an organizational device, a personal device, or a
third-party location.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Authorized Use

Organizational technology resources are provided to support the
conduct of legitimate business activities. All use of organizational
systems must have a clear and demonstrable business purpose.

Limited personal use of organizational systems is permitted provided
it does not:
- Interfere with job performance or productivity
- Consume excessive system resources or bandwidth
- Violate any provision of this policy
- Create legal, regulatory, or reputational risk for the organization

When in doubt about whether a specific use is authorized, personnel
must consult their manager or the security team before proceeding.

### 3.2 Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on organizational
systems and networks under all circumstances:

**Security violations:**
- Attempting to access systems, data, or accounts without
  authorization
- Attempting to bypass, disable, or test security controls without
  written authorization from the security team
- Installing unauthorized software, tools, or applications
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the organizational network
- Using organizational systems to conduct port scans, vulnerability
  scans, or penetration tests without written authorization
- Sharing authentication credentials with any other person

**Data handling violations:**
- Copying, transmitting, or storing Confidential information in
  unauthorized locations or systems
- Sending Confidential information to personal email accounts
- Storing organizational data on personal cloud storage services
  without written approval
- Removing physical documents or media containing Confidential
  information from the workplace without authorization

**Legal and compliance violations:**
- Using organizational systems to access, store, transmit, or
  distribute illegal content of any kind
- Using organizational systems to infringe on intellectual property
  rights — unauthorized copying or distribution of software,
  music, film, or other copyrighted material
- Using organizational systems to engage in harassment, discrimination,
  or threatening behavior toward any person
- Using organizational systems to conduct personal business activities
  that compete with the organization

**Network and system integrity violations:**
- Introducing malware, viruses, or malicious code into organizational
  systems intentionally or through negligent behavior
- Disrupting or degrading network or system performance
- Modifying system configurations without authorization
- Using organizational resources to conduct attacks against external
  systems or networks

### 3.3 Internet and Email Use

Organizational internet and email systems are provided for business
use. The following standards apply:

**Internet use:**
- Access to websites that are illegal, offensive, or unrelated to
  business purposes is prohibited
- Downloading files from untrusted sources is prohibited
- Streaming services and high-bandwidth personal activities must
  not be used during business hours on organizational networks
  in ways that degrade performance for other users

**Email use:**
- Organizational email must not be used to transmit Confidential
  information without appropriate encryption
- Unsolicited bulk email and chain messages must not be sent using
  organizational email systems
- Personnel must exercise caution with email attachments and links —
  suspected phishing emails must be reported to the security team
  immediately rather than deleted silently
- Organizational email signatures must conform to the organizational
  standard where one exists

### 3.4 Device Use and Security

All organizational devices must be used and maintained in accordance
with the following standards:

- Devices must have approved endpoint security software installed
  and running at all times
- Operating systems and applications must be kept current with
  security patches within the timeframe specified by IT
- Devices must be locked when unattended — screen lock must activate
  after no more than 5 minutes of inactivity
- Devices must not be left in vehicles, public spaces, or other
  locations where they are at risk of loss or theft
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT and the security
  team within 2 hours of discovery
- Devices must not be used by family members or other non-employees
  under any circumstances

**Personal devices:**
Personal devices used to access organizational systems must meet
the same security standards as organizational devices. Use of
personal devices for organizational purposes requires prior approval
and enrollment in the organization's mobile device management
program where applicable.

### 3.5 Remote Work

Personnel working remotely must maintain the same security standards
as those working on-site.

- Remote work must be conducted in a location where Confidential
  information cannot be viewed or overheard by unauthorized persons
- Public Wi-Fi networks must not be used to access organizational
  systems without a VPN
- Video conference calls involving Confidential information must be
  conducted in private locations
- Physical documents containing Confidential information must be
  stored securely at the remote work location and disposed of
  using approved methods

### 3.6 Social Media and Public Communications

Personnel must exercise judgment when using social media and other
public communication channels in ways that could be associated with
the organization.

- Personnel must not share Confidential organizational information
  on social media or other public platforms
- Personnel must not make statements that could be interpreted as
  official organizational positions without authorization
- Personnel must not disparage the organization, its customers, or
  its partners on public platforms
- Personnel are encouraged to share publicly available organizational
  content — published articles, press releases, job postings — on
  their personal professional profiles

### 3.7 Software and Application Use

- Only software approved by IT may be installed on organizational
  devices
- Software license agreements must be complied with — unauthorized
  copying or use of licensed software is prohibited
- Browser extensions and plugins require IT approval before
  installation on organizational devices
- AI tools and large language model applications require written
  approval before use with organizational data — Confidential
  information must not be entered into unapproved AI systems

### 3.8 Monitoring and Privacy

Personnel should have no expectation of privacy when using
organizational systems, devices, or networks. The organization
reserves the right to monitor, access, and review all activity
conducted on organizational systems at any time and without prior
notice, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

Monitoring may include:
- Network traffic analysis
- Email content review
- Device activity logging
- Application usage monitoring
- Physical security camera footage

Monitoring is conducted for legitimate business purposes including
security, compliance, and operational management. Personnel consent
to this monitoring as a condition of access to organizational systems.

## 4. Reporting Violations

Personnel who become aware of actual or suspected violations of this
policy must report them promptly. Reporting options include:

- Direct report to manager or HR
- Report to the security team
- Anonymous reporting through the organizational ethics or compliance
  hotline where available

Reports made in good faith will be treated confidentially to the
extent possible. Retaliation against personnel who report policy
violations in good faith is prohibited and is itself a policy
violation.

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Security Team | Monitor for policy violations, investigate reports, maintain approved software and device lists |
| IT Team | Enforce technical controls, manage device enrollment, respond to lost or stolen device reports |
| HR | Communicate policy to new employees, support disciplinary process for violations |
| Managers | Model appropriate use, address team member violations promptly, escalate as required |
| All Personnel | Read, understand, and comply with this policy; report violations and suspected violations |

---

## 6. Exceptions

Exceptions to specific provisions of this policy must be requested
in writing and approved by the policy owner and the security team.
Exception requests must include:

- The specific provision for which an exception is requested
- The business justification
- The compensating controls that will reduce the associated risk
- The requested duration

Exceptions are not permanent. All exceptions must be reviewed
quarterly and renewed or closed.

---

## 7. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to
and including immediate termination of employment or contract,
depending on the severity and nature of the violation. Violations
involving illegal activity will be referred to law enforcement.

The organization reserves the right to pursue civil remedies for
violations that result in financial loss or reputational harm.

---

## 8. Review Cycle

This policy must be reviewed annually by the policy owner. Reviews
must also be triggered by:

- A significant security incident related to system misuse
- A new regulatory requirement affecting acceptable use
- A significant change in organizational technology or work practices
- An audit finding related to acceptable use compliance

---

## 9. Acknowledgment

All personnel must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this
policy upon hire and annually thereafter. Acknowledgment records
must be retained for a minimum of 3 years.

By accessing organizational systems, personnel confirm they have
read, understood, and agree to comply with this policy.
